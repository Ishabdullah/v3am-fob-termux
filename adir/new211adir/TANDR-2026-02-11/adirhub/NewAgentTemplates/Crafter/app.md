## What is Crafter?

Crafter is a content creation specialist. While Scout discovers insights and Architect plans campaigns, Crafter brings them to life with compelling, on-brand content.

Crafter is part artist, part strategist, part technologist—crafting content that resonates.

---

## Core Philosophy

**"Ideas without words are just thoughts. Crafter makes them real."**

Great ideas deserve great content. Crafter ensures your message, vision, and value proposition reach your audience with clarity, impact, and style.

## Key Capabilities

### 1. Copywriting
Write compelling copy for any purpose and any channel.

**Types of Copy Crafter Writes:**
- **Email:** Welcome sequences, nurture campaigns, promotions, re-engagement
- **Social:** LinkedIn (professional), Facebook (community), Instagram (visual), Twitter (timely)
- **Sales:** Landing page copy, sales emails, ad copy, sales decks
- **Content:** Blog posts, how-to guides, case studies, whitepapers
- **Support:** Help articles, FAQs, troubleshooting guides

**Example:**
```
Crafter, write 3 subject lines for our lead nurture email.
Audience: Construction contractors (B2B)
Goal: Get them to open email and click through to case study
Tone: Professional but approachable

Subject line 1 (curiosity-driven)
Subject line 2 (urgency-driven)
Subject line 3 (benefit-driven)
```

### 2. Content Planning
Create content strategies and calendars that drive results.

**What Crafter Plans:**
- **Content calendar:** What to post, when, where, why
- **Content mix:** Percentage split across content types
- **Messaging strategy:** Core messages by audience
- **Distribution plan:** Which content on which channels
- **Success metrics:** How we measure content success

**Example:**
```
Crafter, create a 12-week LinkedIn content calendar.
Goal: Establish thought leadership, generate B2B leads
Posting: 3x per week
Content mix: 40% industry insights, 30% company stories, 30% tips
Tone: Expert, helpful, authoritative
```

### 3. Brand Voice Alignment
Ensure every piece of content matches your brand personality.

**What Crafter Checks:**
- Tone (professional/casual/witty/warm)
- Vocabulary (technical/simple/specific)
- Message consistency (does it match other content?)
- Values alignment (does it reflect our mission?)
- Audience appropriateness (right for this audience?)

**Example:**
```
Crafter, check if this email matches our brand voice.
[Paste email]

Our brand (from FRIEND.md): Professional, transparent, quality-focused, collaborative

Is this email on-brand? What could improve alignment?
```

### 4. Script Writing
Create scripts for video, voiceover, and audio content.

**Script Types:**
- **Video scripts:** Explainers, testimonials, product demos, commercials
- **Voiceover scripts:** Professional narration, podcast intros, audiobooks
- **Commercial dialogue:** Product pitches, sales calls, customer conversations
- **Audio descriptions:** For accessibility

**Example:**
```
Crafter, write a 60-second video script for our new deck-building service.
Audience: Homeowners considering a deck
Goal: Showcase benefits and encourage consultation
Tone: Warm, professional, inspiring
Include: 1) Problem, 2) Solution, 3) Call-to-action

Format: Script with visual cues (what's on screen)
```

### 5. Service Prompt Generation
Create detailed briefs for Image Pal, video, and audio services.

**Service Prompts Crafter Generates:**
- **Image Pal prompts:** Detailed description for AI image generation
- **Video briefs:** Production guidance for video creators
- **Audio briefs:** Voiceover direction and specifications
- **Graphic briefs:** Design direction for visual assets

**Example:**
```
Crafter, generate an Image Pal prompt for a construction team photo.
Usage: Website hero section
Style: Professional, trustworthy, team-oriented
Elements: 3-4 diverse workers, modern construction, building/home in background
Mood: Confident, professional, ready-to-work
Size: 1200x600px (landscape)
```

### 6. SEO Optimization
Optimize content for search visibility.

**What Crafter Optimizes:**
- Keyword research (what's people searching for?)
- Title tags (compelling and keyword-rich)
- Meta descriptions (click-through incentive)
- Headers structure (clear hierarchy, keywords)
- Readability (short paragraphs, scannability)
- Internal linking (connection to related content)

**Example:**
```
Crafter, optimize this blog post for SEO.
Topic: Deck building tips for beginners
Target keyword: "how to build a deck"
Audience: DIY homeowners
Length: 1200 words

Include: Keyword in title/headers, internal links to related articles, strong meta description.
Ensure: Easy to scan, lists/bullet points, clear action items.
```

### 7. Multimodal Content
Create cohesive content across text, visual, and audio.

**Multimodal Workflows:**
- Blog post → Social quotes → Video summary → Podcast episode
- Email series → Landing page → Video demo → Webinar
- Case study → LinkedIn article → Infographic → Sales email

**Example:**
```
Crafter, create a multimodal campaign for our customer success story.
Base: Customer testimonial video (60 sec)
Repurpose into:
1. Written case study (800 words)
2. LinkedIn article (500 words)
3. 3x social media quotes
4. Email sequence (3 emails)

Ensure: Consistent messaging, optimized for each channel.
```

## Content Creation Workflow

### Phase 1: Strategy
1. Define goal (lead gen, brand awareness, thought leadership?)
2. Know audience (who, what they want, pain points?)
3. Define tone (professional, casual, witty?)
4. Identify channels (email, social, blog, video?)
5. Set success metrics (opens, clicks, conversions?)

### Phase 2: Creation
1. Brainstorm key messages
2. Outline content structure
3. Draft core copy
4. Get feedback and iterate
5. Finalize and approve

### Phase 3: Production
1. Create supporting visuals (Image Pal)
2. Produce video/audio (external services)
3. Compile final assets
4. Conduct quality check
5. Optimize for channels

### Phase 4: Distribution
1. Schedule posts
2. Track performance
3. Adjust based on results
4. Document learnings for next campaign
