## Task Template 1: Email Campaign Copywriting

### Objective
Write a complete email sequence for a marketing campaign.

### Input Requirements
- **Campaign goal:** What do we want to achieve? (lead gen, nurture, sale, retention)
- **Audience:** Who are we emailing? (contractors, homeowners, businesses)
- **Sequence type:** How many emails? (3, 5, 7-email?)
- **Timeline:** How spread out? (1 email/day, every 3 days, weekly?)
- **Tone:** Professional? Casual? Friendly?
- **Key message:** What's the main value proposition?

### Output
- Email 1: Opener/Hook
- Email 2: Build value
- Email 3: Social proof/case study
- Email 4: Limited offer/urgency
- Email 5: Last chance/final call
- All with: Subject line, preview text, body copy, CTA

### Example
```
Crafter, write a 5-email nurture sequence.
Target: 50 warm construction leads (contacted recently)
Goal: 20% conversion to sales meeting
Email schedule: Days 1, 3, 7, 14, 21

Email 1 (Welcome): Introduce value, set expectations
Email 2 (Day 3): Success story/case study
Email 3 (Day 7): How we do it (social proof)
Email 4 (Day 14): Limited offer (urgency)
Email 5 (Day 21): Last chance

All subject lines should be compelling and tested.
Tone: Professional but friendly, consultative.
```

## Task Template 3: Landing Page Copy

### Objective
Write compelling landing page copy for a specific offer.

### Input Requirements
- **Offer:** What are we promoting? (free assessment, free consultation, discount)
- **Audience:** Who is landing here?
- **Goal:** What action? (sign up, call, download)
- **Value prop:** Why should they care?
- **Constraints:** Any legal/compliance requirements?

### Output
- Headline (benefit-driven, compelling)
- Subheadline (expand on headline)
- Body copy (3-4 main benefits)
- Social proof (testimonials, stats, logos)
- CTA button (action-oriented)
- Form fields (minimal, only what you need)
- Trust signals (guarantees, credentials)

### Example
```
Crafter, write landing page copy for our "Free Deck Assessment" offer.
Target: Homeowners considering a deck
Offer: Free design consultation (no obligation)
Goal: Get them to book a 15-minute call
Value prop: Professional design + guaranteed quality (CCB licensed)

Sections:
- Hero section (headline + subheadline)
- Problem section (why a deck matters)
- Solution section (what we offer)
- Proof section (testimonials, portfolio)
- CTA button (Book Your Free Assessment)
- Form (just name, email, phone - keep short)

Tone: Friendly, professional, trustworthy
```

## Task Template 4: Sales Email Sequence

### Objective
Write a sales email sequence for B2B prospecting.

### Input Requirements
- **Product/service:** What are we selling?
- **Target:** What size businesses? (SMB, enterprise)
- **Pain point:** What problem do we solve?
- **Sequence:** How many emails? (3, 5)
- **Timeline:** Space between emails?

### Output
- Email 1: Initial value proposition
- Email 2: Social proof / case study
- Email 3: Limited offer or personalization
- Email 4 (optional): Final call / urgency
- Email 5 (optional): Check-in if no response

### Example
```
Crafter, write a 4-email B2B sales sequence.
Product: Construction management software
Target: Small construction companies (5-50 people)
Pain point: Inefficient project management, cost overruns

Email 1 (Introduction): "I noticed you're in construction..."
Email 2 (Value): "Here's how similar companies saved 15% on costs"
Email 3 (Proof): Success story from comparable company
Email 4 (Offer): "Special 30-day trial + bonus template"

Personalization: Include their company name, industry
Tone: Professional, consultative, non-salesy
```
